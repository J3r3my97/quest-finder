# Massachusetts Procurement Data Sources Inventory

## Overview

This document catalogs procurement data sources across Massachusetts for building a contract opportunity aggregation platform. Sources are organized by entity type and include technical notes on data access methods.

---

## 1. STATE LEVEL

### COMMBUYS (Primary State Portal)
- **URL**: https://www.commbuys.com/bso/
- **Coverage**: All Commonwealth of Massachusetts executive department procurements
- **Data Access**: 
  - Public search available without login
  - Email notifications available with free seller registration
  - Browse open bids, active contracts/blankets
- **Format**: Web-based, likely scrapeable HTML tables
- **Update Frequency**: Real-time posting
- **Notes**: This is THE official state procurement system. Many local entities also post here. Has UNSPSC commodity codes for categorization.

### Massachusetts Central Register
- **URL**: Available through mass.gov (paid subscription)
- **Coverage**: State, county, and municipal solicitations for design, construction, and reconstruction of public facilities
- **Data Access**: Paid subscription required
- **Notes**: Good for construction-related bids

### Massachusetts Goods and Services Bulletin
- **URL**: Available through mass.gov (paid subscription)  
- **Coverage**: Weekly publication with municipal procurement solicitations
- **Data Access**: Paid subscription required

### DCAMM (Division of Capital Asset Management & Maintenance)
- **URL**: https://www.bidexpress.com/businesses/10279/home
- **Coverage**: State building construction, renovation projects
- **Data Access**: BidExpress platform (requires registration)
- **Notes**: Large construction contracts across state facilities

## 2. MAJOR CITIES

### Boston
- **Primary Portal**: https://www.boston.gov/bid-listings
- **Supplier Portal**: https://procurement.boston.gov/
- **Coverage**: All City of Boston bids and RFPs
- **Data Access**: 
  - Public bid listings viewable online
  - Supplier portal for bid submission (registration required)
  - Email notifications available
- **Format**: HTML listings, PDFs for bid documents
- **Notes**: Boston uses its own supplier portal separate from COMMBUYS. Strong M/WBE programs with set-asides.

### Boston Planning & Development Agency (BPDA)
- **URL**: http://www.bostonplans.org/procurement/procurement-portal
- **Coverage**: BPDA-specific RFQs, RFPs, solicitations
- **Data Access**: Web portal

### Cambridge
- **Primary Portal**: https://www2.cambridgema.gov/purchasing/listBids.cfm
- **eProcurement System**: Referenced on site
- **Coverage**: City of Cambridge bids (IFBs, RFPs, construction)
- **Data Access**: 
  - Public listings by category (Construction, IFB, RFP)
  - Free vendor registration for notifications
- **Format**: HTML tables, downloadable documents
- **Notes**: Separate listings for different bid types

### Worcester
- **URL**: https://www.worcesterma.gov/finance/purchasing-bids
- **Coverage**: City of Worcester open bids, quotes, RFPs
- **Data Access**:
  - WorcBids email alert system (free registration)
  - Online bid database searchable for 3 years
- **Format**: Web-based with downloadable documents
- **Notes**: Has closed bids archive and search functionality

### Springfield
- **URL**: https://www.springfield-ma.gov/finance/procurement-bids/
- **Coverage**: City of Springfield solicitations
- **Data Access**: 
  - Public listings on city website
  - Also advertises on COMMBUYS
- **Format**: HTML listings with deadlines clearly shown

## 3. TRANSIT & UTILITIES

### MBTA (Massachusetts Bay Transportation Authority)
- **Primary URL**: https://www.mbta.com/business/bids-solicitations
- **Materials Management**: https://bc.mbta.com/business_center/bidding_solicitations/
- **Capital Projects**: https://www.bidexpress.com/businesses/83754/home
- **Coverage**: 
  - General goods/services (uses COMMBUYS statewide contracts)
  - Small contracts <$50K (FairMarkIT platform)
  - Large contracts >$50K (internal procurement)
  - Capital delivery projects (design, construction)
- **Data Access**:
  - Multiple platforms depending on contract type
  - FairMarkIT for small purchases (no registration needed to bid)
  - BidExpress for capital projects
- **Notes**: Has DBE and SBE programs. ~15,000 purchases/year under $50K.

### Boston Water and Sewer Commission (BWSC)
- **URL**: https://www.bwsc.org/builders-contractors/bidding-opportunities
- **Portal**: BidNet Direct portal for BWSC
- **Coverage**: Water/sewer infrastructure, goods, services
- **Data Access**: BidNet Direct account required for documents
- **Notes**: Encourages M/WBE participation

### MWRA (Massachusetts Water Resources Authority)
- **Supplier Portal**: https://supplier.mwra.com (referenced in search results)
- **Coverage**: Wholesale water and sewer services for 61 municipalities
- **Data Access**: MWRA Supplier Portal
- **Notes**: Serves 3.1 million people, significant procurement volume

### Eversource / National Grid / Unitil
- **Coverage**: Major utility procurements (energy, storage, infrastructure)
- **Notes**: Large-scale clean energy procurements handled through state processes (DOER). Not typical small business opportunities but worth monitoring for subcontracting.

## 4. EDUCATION

### Boston Public Schools
- **URL**: Part of Boston's supplier portal (boston.gov/procurement)
- **Coverage**: BPS supplies, services, professional development
- **Data Access**: Through City of Boston supplier portal
- **Notes**: Follows M.G.L. c.30B. Some exemptions for textbooks, professional development.

### UMass System
- **Sourcing Portal**: https://bids.sciquest.com/apps/Router/PublicEvent?CustomerOrg=UMass
- **UPST Info**: https://www.umassp.edu/upst/suppliers-doing-business-umass
- **Construction**: https://biddocs.com/listings (for some projects)
- **UMass Building Authority**: https://www.umassba.org/solicitations
- **Coverage**: All UMass campuses (Amherst, Boston, Lowell, Dartmouth, Medical)
- **Data Access**: 
  - Jaggaer eSourcing platform
  - Free supplier registration
- **Notes**: ~$1 billion annual third-party spend. Uses MHEC contracts. Strong supplier diversity program.

### Community Colleges (15 colleges)
- **Primary Method**: Most use COMMBUYS for formal bids
- **Consortia**: Massachusetts Higher Education Consortium (MHEC), E&I Cooperative
- **Example Colleges**:
  - Cape Cod CC: https://www.capecod.edu/procurement/
  - Quinsigamond CC: https://www.qcc.edu/about-qcc/administration/procurement-services
  - Berkshire CC: https://www.berkshirecc.edu/about-bcc/Procurement/
  - Holyoke CC: Uses COMMBUYS for notifications
  - Springfield Tech CC: https://www.stcc.edu/about-stcc/purchasing/
- **Notes**: Follow M.G.L. c.30B. Bids >$50K typically competitive. Many use state contracts.

## 5. HEALTHCARE

### Mass General Brigham (formerly Partners HealthCare)
- **URL**: https://www.massgeneralbrigham.org/en/supplier-vendor-information
- **Coverage**: Medical supplies, services, construction across MGB system
- **Data Access**: 
  - Vendor credentialing through Green Security
  - Impact Purchase Program (launched 2025) for local/diverse businesses
- **Notes**: ~$20 billion operating revenue. Strong push for local/M/WBE suppliers. Not public procurement (private non-profit) but significant opportunity.

### UMass Memorial Health
- **URL**: https://www.ummhealth.org/about-us/anchor-mission/purchasing
- **Coverage**: Healthcare supplies and services
- **Notes**: Impact Purchasing Commitment to increase M/WBE spend

## 6. COUNTIES

Massachusetts counties have limited procurement authority (most was abolished). County-level procurement is minimal but some exists:

### Norfolk County
- **URL**: https://www.norfolkcounty.org/purchasing_department/bid_opportunities.php
- **Coverage**: Limited county services

### Note on Counties
Most procurement in Massachusetts happens at the municipal (city/town) level rather than county level. Focus efforts on cities and towns.

## 7. MUNICIPALITIES (Towns)

Many Massachusetts towns use third-party e-procurement platforms:

### Common Platforms Used by MA Towns

**OpenGov Procurement**
- Example: Provincetown (https://www.provincetown-ma.gov/37/Procurement)
- Example: Nantucket, Hingham, others
- Features: Electronic bidding, vendor management, notifications

**BidNet Direct**
- URL: https://www.bidnetdirect.com/massachusetts
- Coverage: Aggregates many MA municipal bids
- Access: Registration required, some free features

### Individual Town Examples
- **Nantucket**: https://www.nantucket-ma.gov/199/Procurement (uses OpenGov)
- **Watertown**: Posts on town website
- Many small towns: Post to COMMBUYS or local website only

## 8. CERTIFICATIONS

### Massachusetts Supplier Diversity Office (SDO)
- **URL**: https://www.mass.gov/certification-program-for-sdo
- **Portal**: SDO Certification Portal
- **Certifications Offered**:
  - MBE (Minority Business Enterprise)
  - WBE (Women Business Enterprise)
  - VBE (Veteran Business Enterprise)
  - PBE (Portuguese Business Enterprise)
- **Third-Party Recognition**:
  - SDVOBE (Service-Disabled Veteran) - via Disability:IN
  - LGBTBE - via NGLCC
  - DOBE (Disability-Owned) - via Disability:IN
- **Cross-Certification**: 
  - GNEMSDC MBE → SDO MBE (expedited)
  - WBENC/CWE WBE → SDO WBE (expedited)
  - Federal DBE → SDO certifications
- **Notes**: Free certification. Pre-certification webcast required. 30-60 day processing.

### City of Boston Supplier Diversity Program
- **URL**: Part of boston.gov/procurement
- **Coverage**: Boston-specific M/WBE certification
- **Notes**: Separate from state SDO but often cross-referenced

### Federal DBE/UCP
- **Certifying Agency**: SDO administers Massachusetts UCP
- **Coverage**: Required for USDOT-funded projects (MBTA, MassDOT)

## 9. AGGREGATOR PLATFORMS

- **URL**: https://www.bidnetdirect.com/massachusetts
- **Coverage**: Aggregates state and local government bids
- **Access**: Registration required
- **Pricing**: Free basic access, premium tiers available

### MassBids.net
- **URL**: https://www.massbids.net/
- **Coverage**: Construction bids, government bids, RFPs
- **Notes**: Good for construction-specific opportunities

### InstantMarkets
- **URL**: https://www.instantmarkets.com
- **Coverage**: National including MA bids

### FindRFP
- **URL**: https://www.findrfp.com/State-RFP-Bid/Government-RFPS-bids-Massachusetts-MA.aspx
- **Coverage**: MA state and local RFPs

### BidExpress
- **URL**: https://www.bidexpress.com

## 12. COMPLIANCE DOCUMENTS (Common Requirements)

Based on research, most MA local contracts require:
- W-9
- Certificate of Insurance (COI)
- Business license / Certificate of Good Standing
- CORI/background checks (for some services)
- SDO certification (for set-aside contracts)
- Bonding (for construction)
- Living Wage compliance (Cambridge, Boston)

## Technical Notes for Scraping

### COMMBUYS
- Session-based browsing
- Can browse open bids without login
- Tables with bid details extractable
- PDF attachments for full bid documents

### Municipal Sites
- Generally static HTML or simple CMSs
- Tables, lists with deadlines
- PDF attachments common
- Update patterns vary (check "last modified" or specific posting dates)

### Change Detection Strategy
- Hash page content for change detection
- Track new PDF links
- Monitor bid deadline fields
- RSS/email where available as primary, scraping as backup
